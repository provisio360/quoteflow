# QuoteFlow

A multi-tenant platform for running pricing studies: researchers enter price quotes, analysts review and approve them, and clients view results. It replaces the Excel-and-email back-and-forth of traditional pricing research.

## Language

**Pricing Study**:
A top-level project container owned by one tenant and scoped to one end-client. It holds the set of items to be priced, the people working on it, and the price quotes collected against those items.
_Avoid_: Project, survey, campaign

**Benchmark Item**:
The client's part to be price-benchmarked, defined once and carrying the client's guidance: country, item/part description **and** an optional secondary description, **Client Item Number** (the key) **and** an optional secondary item number, configuration comment, order quantity, the machine/model it belongs to — the **Client Source Unit** (e.g. `BRC8T450X`) plus an optional **Source Unit Identifier** — an optional **Client Category** and **Client Item Offering** (Standard/Premium), a per-item [[QC Threshold]] (optional, falls back to the study default), a [[Client Price]] seed (the `{currency, price, quantity}` trio, derived to USD/unit), a **Required Quotes** count, and an optional [[Required Competitors]] list. The descriptive fields beyond the Client Item Number are nullable. A Benchmark Item has many [[Quote Line]]s (one per item within each covering [[Market Quote]]). Uniquely identified within a study by **Client Item Number + country** (the key a spreadsheet re-import upserts on, ADR-0009); the secondary numbers and source unit are descriptive, never identity.
_Avoid_: Target part, subject part, line item, SKU

**Client Price**:
The expected **USD price-per-unit** benchmark for a Benchmark Item. The brief supplies it as three columns — a price, in a currency, at a basis quantity (`Client Item Price`, `Client Item Price Currency`, `Client Item Price Quantity`, the last **distinct** from the item's order [[quantity|Client Item Quantity]]) — and the system **derives** USD/unit at import as `price ÷ priceQuantity` (v1 requires the currency to be **USD**; a non-USD brief price is an import validation error). The raw three are kept only as **seed provenance**; the derived USD/unit is the operative value, **owned and maintained by the analyst** thereafter — the brief is *not* its source of truth, so a re-import never overwrites a Client Price the analyst owns; it only **seeds** one onto an item still genuinely **unpriced** (never seeded and never analyst-cleared), back-filling items that pre-date their price. The analyst corrects an existing value in-app (ADR-0015, ADR-0027, ADR-0030). Used purely as an internal QC reference: a [[Quote Line]] whose USD price-per-unit differs from it by more than the item's [[QC Threshold]] is **flagged** (see [[Price Flag]]). **May be unset** — an item the client never priced has no Client Price and is *not comparable*, raising no flag (parallel to an unconverted [[Quote Line]]). Hidden from researchers (to avoid biasing the quotes they collect) and never shown to clients or included in client exports. Powers the internal analyst QC view, not any client-facing dashboard.

**QC Threshold**:
The fraction that defines how far a [[Quote Line]]'s USD price-per-unit may diverge from its [[Benchmark Item]]'s [[Client Price]] before the line is flagged for analyst attention. Stored as a **fraction** (e.g. `0.8` = 80%) — **both** the per-item value and the study default share this single unit, so the `per-item ?? study` fallback never mixes scales — matching the symmetric relative difference measure `|USD-per-unit − ClientPrice| / ((USD-per-unit + ClientPrice) / 2)` it is compared against. **Per-item with a study default**: each Benchmark Item may carry its own threshold (the client can tighten tolerance on critical parts — it rides in on the bulk upload, `Price Difference Threshold`), and where an item sets none it falls back to the study's threshold, defined by the [[Engagement Manager]] or [[Analyst]] at study setup. Every item therefore always has *a* threshold (the study one is required — a study without it is mis-configured); the per-item value only overrides it. The measure is relative, so one fraction works across cheap and expensive parts. Amends ADR-0014 (which set one threshold per study).
_Avoid_: tolerance, variance limit, percentage points

**Price Flag**:
The QC signal raised on a [[Quote Line]] whose USD price-per-unit diverges from its [[Benchmark Item]]'s [[Client Price]] by more than the item's [[QC Threshold]]. Only computable once the line's parent [[Market Quote]] is converted **and** its Benchmark Item has a [[Client Price]] (lacking either, the line is *not comparable*, never flagged). The flag is **advisory, not a hard block** — but approving a flagged line requires the author's [[Justification]] first. The analyst sees the flag and its direction (higher/lower than expected); the researcher never does (the [[Client Price]] is hidden from them, ADR-0003).
_Avoid_: alert, warning, exception

**Justification**:
The author-supplied explanation that a flagged [[Quote Line]]'s price is genuinely correct despite diverging from the [[Client Price]]. Required before a flagged line can be approved, and gathered only by returning the line to its author (who is told the direction of the divergence, never the benchmark value). Distinct from the line's free-text **notes**: a justification specifically answers a [[Price Flag]]. Persists on the line across resubmission (unlike a rejection reason, which is cleared on resubmit).
_Avoid_: explanation, comment, reason

**Market Quote**:
A single dealer's quotation document a [[Researcher]] collects: one [[Distributor/Dealer]] (source), one **Date Quote Received**, and one local currency, covering a *subset* of a [[Country]]'s [[Benchmark Item]]s — from a single item up to all of them. A dealer who cannot price the whole list simply yields fewer [[Quote Line]]s; there is **no separate by-row entity** — a one-line Market Quote *is* the by-row case. Only **source, date, and currency** truly define the document (they drive the single pinned [[Exchange Rate]]); the [[Competitor]] brand and the Paper-Quote flag are **line-level** (usually uniform across a document and defaulted once at entry, but they may vary line to line). The document holds the facts shared by all its lines (source name/location/URL, date, currency) and has many [[Quote Line]]s, one per Benchmark Item it prices. Its identity is its [[Market Quote Number]], not (source, date) — the same dealer may file two documents on one date. It is the unit a client counts toward an item's [[Required Quotes]] (N dealer quotes per item). In everyday speech **"a quote" is this document**, not a single line.
_Avoid_: Quote (for a single line), dealer quote, RFQ, bid

**Quote Line**:
One competitive data point **within a [[Market Quote]]**: the [[Competitor]]'s equivalent part priced against one [[Benchmark Item]] — its **Competitor brand**, competitor part identifiers/description, [[Quantity Quoted]], the dealer's quoted price (in the parent's local currency), stock status, lead time (value + unit), up to two warranties (value + unit each), discount (a chain: **available** Yes/No → if available, a free-text **type** (what kind of discount is on offer, captured even when not applied) **and** whether it was **applied** to the quote Yes/No → if applied, a **value** as a recorded **percentage** (e.g. `15` = 15%); all advisory metadata, the value **never applied to the price**, which is already discount-inclusive), landed cost (flag + note), notes, a **Confidence Code** (High/Moderate/Low), and a **Paper-Quote** flag. Exactly one line per Benchmark Item within a given Market Quote. The brand, paper-quote flag, and confidence are usually uniform across a document (defaulted once at entry) but **may vary per line**; the dealer, date, and currency are *not* on the line — they live on the parent Market Quote. Its **price** is always the dealer's final quoted price — discount-inclusive when the dealer states a discount was applied (not all lines have one). This is what the v1 walking-skeleton called a flat "Quote".
_Avoid_: Quote, Row, price entry, bid

**Client**:
An end-client company that commissions pricing studies. The client is the **tenant**: its studies, benchmark items, and quotes are isolated from every other client. A Client is a company, not a person — it never logs in. The humans who log in on its behalf are **Client Users**.
_Avoid_: Customer, account, organization

**Client User**:
A person who logs in on behalf of exactly one Client (tenant). The only external (non-staff) user. Viewer-only and tenant-bound: sees only that Client's released data, never another tenant's, and has no intra-client roles in v1 (every Client User of a tenant has the same view). Many Client Users may belong to one Client. Distinct from the **Client** itself, which is the company/tenant and never logs in.
_Avoid_: Client (when you mean the person), customer contact, account user

**Engagement Manager**:
Internal staff member who runs a study and assigns researchers to Countries (a Country may have several). Sets the pool who may work each Country.
_Avoid_: Project manager, study owner

**Researcher**:
Internal staff member who collects quotes by contacting distributors/dealers. Works within Countries the Engagement Manager assigned them to, and self-assigns individual Benchmark Items. The lead on a given Benchmark Item is its _primary researcher_; others may still contribute quotes to the same item.
_Avoid_: Agent, collector

**Analyst**:
Internal staff member who performs quality checks on quotes and approves them before clients see them. Can also create a [[Pricing Study]]: study **creation** is a shared internal-setup capability held by Engagement Managers and Analysts (not the Admin, who remains user-administration only), distinct from **running** a study, which stays the Engagement Manager's job.
_Avoid_: Reviewer, QA

**Admin**:
Internal staff member responsible for user administration: invites users, assigns internal roles, and binds client users to their tenant. The root of the invite tree — the only role that can mint other internal staff (including other Admins) and client users. Like all internal staff, an Admin is **not tenant-scoped**. User-administration authority only; being an Admin does not by itself grant Engagement Manager, Researcher, or Analyst capabilities (those are separate role assignments).
_Avoid_: Superuser, owner, operator

**Invite**:
An Admin's single-use, expiring offer to create one account for a specific email, carrying the bindings chosen up front — either an internal **role** or a **Client** (tenant). There is no other way to obtain an account (no self-signup). Accepting an Invite (setting a password via the emailed link) both creates the account and serves as **email verification** — possession of the link proves inbox control, so no separate verification step exists. A pending Invite is not yet a [[Client User]] or staff account: it cannot authenticate until accepted. Invites can be revoked or resent by an Admin and expire after a configurable window.
_Avoid_: Signup, registration, activation link

**Market Quote Number**:
The reference label for a [[Market Quote]], a monotonic counter **per (study, country)** — it restarts each market, which is why it is the *Market* Quote Number ("Brazil quote 3"). Assigned when a [[Researcher]] starts a document and retained for life. Counts toward an item's [[Required Quotes]] (N documents per item). Abandoning (hard-deleting) an unsubmitted Market Quote leaves a permanent gap in the market's sequence, never reused. Supersedes the v1 per-Benchmark-Item quote number (ADR-0010).
_Avoid_: Row Id, line number

**Quote Line Number**:
The citable reference for a single [[Quote Line]] — a **stored, stable** monotonic counter **flat per (study, country)** (1…N across the whole market, e.g. 1…144 for 6 documents × 24 items). Assigned when the line is created and **never changes** (it survives re-sort and re-export, unlike the spreadsheet "Row Id" it replaces, which was a render-time row position). Retained through a line's [[Rejected]] → revise → resubmit loop; a hard-deleted line leaves a permanent gap, never reused. This is the handle people mean by "Row 87". Distinct from the [[Market Quote Number]] (which labels the document); a line is located by its market quote **and** its [[Benchmark Item]], and carries this number as its durable identity.
_Avoid_: Row Id (when you mean the stable identity), display row, sheet position

**Country**:
The geographic focus of a Benchmark Item's pricing and the unit of client release.
_Avoid_: Market, segment, region, territory

**Country Assignment**:
The Engagement Manager's act of putting a [[Researcher]] onto a Country within a study — the pool allowed to work that Country. A Country may have several assigned researchers, and a researcher may be assigned across many studies. A Country is only assignable once it has Benchmark Items (the brief defines a study's countries), and assignment is **additive**: it only ever adds researchers, never removes them. Distinct from a researcher then self-assigning an individual [[Benchmark Item]] within a Country (becoming its [[Primary Researcher]]), which is a separate, later act.
_Avoid_: Allocation, posting

**Primary Researcher**:
The single lead [[Researcher]] on a [[Benchmark Item]], established when a researcher **self-assigns** that item. Self-assignment is only permitted within a Country the researcher already has a [[Country Assignment]] to, and is **first-come**: an item has at most one primary researcher and the lead cannot be taken over once claimed (re-assignment/handoff is out of scope here). Being primary is a *lead* designation, not a lock on the item — other researchers may still contribute [[Quote Line]]s (via their own [[Market Quote]]s) to the same item without becoming primary. An item may have no primary researcher (unclaimed).
_Avoid_: Owner, assignee, item owner

**Required Quotes**:
The per-Benchmark-Item count of approved **dealer quotes** needed before its Country can be released — counted as distinct [[Market Quote]]s with an [[Approved / Rejected|approved]] [[Quote Line]] for the item (N dealers priced it), not a count of lines. Set by the client up front and carried as a column in the bulk upload; varies per item, so sparse-source parts simply specify a lower number (no analyst override exists). May be **zero** — an item that needs no quotes, whose Country can release without any approved line for it.

**Required Competitors**:
A per-Benchmark-Item list (up to ten, `Required Competitor 1…10` on the bulk upload) of [[Competitor]] brands the client wants benchmarked for that item — a brand is "covered" when some [[Quote Line]] for the item names it (a line's competitor is its [[Market Quote]]'s brand). **Advisory in v1**, not a release gate: captured and surfaced to [[Researcher]]s and [[Analyst]]s as coverage guidance (and an optional coverage indicator), but [[Release Eligibility]] is **not** additionally gated on it — only [[Required Quotes]] count and the no-in-flight rule gate release. Often **empty** (no required brands), in which case it constrains nothing. A hard coverage gate is deferred (brand names are free text; matching would need its own normalization).
_Avoid_: required brands gate, mandatory competitors

**Released**:
The state of a Country whose approved [[Quote Line]]s have been made visible to the client, as a set. Release is scoped to a **(study, country)** pair, not a bare country name — the same country recurs across studies and each is released independently (ADR-0016). The analyst manually releases a Country, gated by a system-enforced precondition (its [[Release Eligibility]]): every Benchmark Item in it has at least its [[Required Quotes]] satisfied (that many distinct [[Market Quote]]s with an approved line for the item), and no line is still **in-flight** (a [[Draft]] or [[Submitted]] line — a [[Rejected]] one does *not* block). A released Country can be reopened (pulling it back from client view) and re-released; the client's view always reflects only currently-released data. Reopen is a **visibility lever only** — it never changes any line's state, so re-release needs no re-approval. Per-line approval alone never exposes a line.

**Release Eligibility**:
The precondition that decides whether a Country *may* be released: every Benchmark Item has at least its [[Required Quotes]] satisfied (that many distinct [[Market Quote]]s with an approved [[Quote Line]] for the item) **and** no item has an in-flight ([[Draft]]/[[Submitted]]) line. A pure, derived judgement — never a stored flag — evaluated fresh from the Country's items at the moment of release (and re-checked inside the release itself, so a race can't release a no-longer-eligible Country). A Country with no Benchmark Items is *not* eligible (releasing nothing is meaningless). Distinct from [[Released]], which is the resulting persisted state; eligibility is the gate, Released is the outcome.
_Avoid_: releasable flag, completion status

**Competitor Price Range**:
The competitive spread reported for a [[Benchmark Item]]: the **min / median / max** [[Converted USD Price / USD Price per Unit|USD price per unit]] across that item's **released + approved** [[Quote Line]]s. That set — Approved lines whose Country is currently [[Released]] — is the *only* population any dashboard aggregates (#14), always within a single [[Pricing Study]]. Each line is one data point: no collapsing by [[Competitor]] or dealer first (a heavily-sourced competitor legitimately weights the spread), and a line with no per-unit figure is excluded. The **median** is the mean of the middle pair for an even-sized population. An item left with **no usable data points** yields an explicit *no-data* result, not zeros and not a vanished row — it was released, so the client still sees it. Surfaces three ways: the client **range view** (min/median/max per item); the client **by-competitor breakdown**, the same range partitioned by [[Competitor]] within the item (keyed on each line's brand; a null brand forms an explicit *unspecified* group); and the internal **benchmark comparison**, the same range set beside the item's [[Client Price]] — *not comparable* when the item has none (mirroring [[Price Flag]]), and **never shown to clients** (ADR-0003). The internal comparison shares the client population (released + approved, never pre-release) — pre-release QC stays per-line via the [[Price Flag]].
_Avoid_: price spread, min-max band, competitor analysis

**Quantity Quoted**:
The quantity in which the distributor/dealer actually sells the item, recorded on a [[Quote Line]] (the `Competitor Item Quantity`). Drives USD Price per Unit. Distinct from the client's own quantity carried on the Benchmark Item.
_Avoid_: Competitor quantity

**Exchange Rate**:
The local-currency-to-USD rate applied to a [[Market Quote]]'s lines. Auto-fetched as the historical rate for the **Market Quote's** Date Quote Received and pinned to the **Market Quote** — one rate for every [[Quote Line]] in the document — so conversions never shift. The fetch is **deferred to a background worker** (see [[Conversion Status]]), which only attempts a Market Quote once its Date Quote Received has fully closed in **UTC** (`dateQuoteReceived < current UTC date`) — before then the historical rate for that date is not yet published, and attempting it would wrongly pin the prior day's rate. On a market-closed date (weekend/holiday) the nearest prior business day's rate is used, and the date actually used is stored. Not entered by researchers; an analyst may set it manually for the whole document (a single audited **manual-rate-override** event per Market Quote, ADR-0023) for currencies the provider doesn't cover.

**Converted USD Price / USD Price per Unit**:
System-computed values on a [[Quote Line]]: the line's local price converted to USD (at its parent [[Market Quote]]'s one pinned rate), and that figure divided by [[Quantity Quoted]]. Always derived, never hand-entered. USD is currently the fixed conversion target.

**Conversion Status**:
Where a **[[Market Quote]]** stands in pinning its [[Exchange Rate]] — one status for the whole document, not per line. A Market Quote is *unconverted* (null) until it is **submitted** (while its [[Quote Line]]s are still [[Draft]]); conversion is never attempted before submit. The moment the document is submitted (its lines move to [[Submitted]] in bulk) it becomes **pending** — conversion is **deferred by default** to a background worker, not fetched at submit (the historical rate for the document's date may not even be published until that date has closed). So `pending` means "no USD figures yet, awaiting the worker," covering both the not-yet-attempted case and the attempted-but-unresolved cases (provider unreachable, no rate within the look-back window, or a currency the provider doesn't cover awaiting a manual rate). While pending, approval of **every line** in the document is blocked. The worker resolves pending to **auto** (a provider rate was found and pinned) or it stays pending until a later run succeeds or an analyst supplies a **manual** rate (a currency the provider doesn't cover). `auto` and `manual` both carry pinned USD figures (each line's derived from the one document rate); the distinction is provenance, and a `manual` rate is sticky — the background worker never overwrites it. Invariant: **null ⇔ not-yet-submitted; once submitted, always pending → auto/manual**. The [[Quote Lifecycle]]'s revise loop does **not** re-pin: a [[Quote Line]] sent back to [[Draft]] and resubmitted keeps its parent's already-pinned rate (the date lives on the document, so a revised line's date cannot change) — the document is converted once and its rate stands. "No re-pin" means the **rate** is reused, not that the line's USD figure is frozen: resubmitting a revised line into an already-`auto`/`manual` document **re-derives** that line's [[Converted USD Price / USD Price per Unit|USD figures]] from the pinned rate, so a corrected price never leaves a stale USD behind (a resubmit into a still-`pending` document needs no re-derivation — the worker fills every line when it pins).
_Avoid_: converted/not-converted (loses the pending and provenance distinctions); "fetched at submit" (conversion is deferred to the worker)

**Draft**:
The state a [[Quote Line]] starts in: a researcher's in-progress working copy, saveable with partial data so collection can be interrupted and resumed. No field is required to hold a Draft; required-field validation applies only on the move to [[Submitted]]. A Draft is internal and pre-review — never in the analyst's queue and never client-visible. (Abandoning an unsubmitted [[Market Quote]] is what leaves a permanent gap in the [[Market Quote Number]] sequence; a discarded line leaves a gap in the [[Quote Line Number]] sequence.)
_Avoid_: Incomplete, pending, unsaved

**Quote Lifecycle**:
The states a **[[Quote Line]]** moves through — **Draft → Submitted → Approved / Rejected**, with a **Rejected → Draft** revise loop — modeled as a single state machine. State lives on the **line**, never on the [[Market Quote]]: a Market Quote has no verdict of its own, only a roll-up of its lines' states. The researcher's **Draft → Submitted** move is taken in **bulk** over a Market Quote (all its [[Draft]] lines transition together — **all-or-nothing**: if any one is missing a required-to-submit field, none move and the researcher is told which lines lack what — and the document's [[Exchange Rate]] pins once at that moment); every analyst verdict and the revise loop are then **per line** — there is no wholesale accept/reject of a dealer's document. The analyst verdicts and the revise loop are the same machine's transitions:
- **approve** (Submitted → Approved): blocked while the line's parent [[Market Quote]]'s [[Conversion Status]] is pending (no line is approved without a real USD figure), and — if the line is flagged (see [[Price Flag]]) — blocked until its author has supplied a [[Justification]].
- **reject** (Submitted → Rejected): requires a reason. The same edge also carries the analyst's act of returning a flagged line to its author **for justification** — the reason states only the direction ("higher/lower than expected"), never the [[Client Price]] value (ADR-0003).
- **revise** (Rejected → Draft): the **only** way out of Rejected, performed by the line's author. The revised line is a Draft again and re-enters the Draft → Submitted path keeping its [[Quote Line Number]] (it keeps its parent's already-pinned rate — see [[Conversion Status]]).

Every legal move is defined in one place; an undefined move (e.g. editing an Approved line, or approving a Draft) is rejected, not silently allowed.

**Submitted**:
A [[Quote Line]] a researcher has marked done and sent to the analyst's review queue, reached in **bulk** when its parent [[Market Quote]] is submitted. Internal-only — "submitted" never means client-visible. The transition out of [[Draft]] is gated: a line can only become Submitted once all required-to-submit fields are present (the bulk submit validates every line in the document).

**Approved / Rejected**:
An [[Analyst]]'s **per-line** verdict (never a wholesale verdict on a [[Market Quote]]). Approved lines await Country release. A rejected line returns to its **author** — the [[Researcher]] who created the parent document (`createdById`), not necessarily the item's [[Primary Researcher]] — carrying the rejection reason. The author may then **revise** it (back to [[Draft]], same [[Quote Line Number]]) and resubmit; only the latest rejection reason is retained. The review back-and-forth is never visible to the client.

**Competitor**:
The rival company and brand whose equivalent part is being priced against the client's Benchmark Item.

**Distributor/Dealer**:
The source a researcher contacts for a [[Market Quote]] — the company that sells the competitor's part and would ship it to the customer. Carries a name, physical location, and URL, all held on the Market Quote (one source per document). Its physical location is split into a validated **[[Dealer Country]]** and a free-text **dealer locality** (city/state/province/region), superseding the old single free-text "Dealer location".
_Avoid_: Vendor, supplier, source

**Dealer Country**:
The validated country a [[Distributor/Dealer]] is physically located in, held on the [[Market Quote]] as a canonical ISO 3166-1 short name (the same vocabulary the market [[Country]] validates against). **Distinct from [[Country]]** — that is the *market* being priced (the unit of client release); Dealer Country is merely *where the source sits* and a dealer may be in a different country than the market it quotes for. Its sole behavioural role: it supplies the **default selection** for the document's local [[currency|Exchange Rate]] (the country's local currency), which the researcher may override to another single currency — the document still holds exactly **one** currency (ADR-0004 unchanged). Purely descriptive provenance otherwise: it never scopes numbering, release, or tenant isolation.
_Avoid_: Country (the market), source location, region

**Export**:
A downloadable spreadsheet of a [[Pricing Study]]'s data (Excel only in v1 — no PDF). Comes in two flavours distinguished by who runs it and what it may contain: the [[Client Export]] and the [[Internal Export]]. Both respect [[Client]] tenant isolation absolutely. There is no aggregate-vs-detail divide in the *format* — both are workbooks; the divide is in the **population and columns** each flavour is allowed.
_Avoid_: Download, report, dump

**Client Export**:
The workbook a [[Client User]] downloads of their own tenant's **released + approved** data — the same population every client dashboard aggregates ([[Competitor Price Range]]). Two sheets: a detail sheet (one row per released+approved [[Quote Line]], the client_final_report shape — line fields plus its parent document's source/date/currency/converted figures, never the [[Client Price]] or Paper-Quote flag) and a summary sheet (per-[[Benchmark Item]] min/median/max). **Never includes [[Client Price]]** (ADR-0003) and never crosses tenants. Runnable by a [[Client User]] (own tenant) and by internal [[Engagement Manager]]/[[Analyst]]/[[Admin]] previewing the client's own output — but **never a [[Researcher]]**: the released "answer" workbook is a side door around their assigned-country read boundary (#64, ADR-0025), so they are refused (HTTP 403), extending the anti-anchoring block the client dashboard already enforces (#63). Not audited — a tenant pulling its own released data is not a sensitive access.
_Avoid_: Client report, released export

**Internal Export**:
The fuller workbook an [[Analyst]] or [[Engagement Manager]] downloads of a whole study: **every non-[[Draft]] [[Quote Line]]** ([[Submitted]]/[[Approved]]/[[Rejected]]) across **all countries** regardless of release, the analyst_tracker shape — line fields plus the parent document's source/date/currency/converted figures, *including* the [[Client Price]] (USD/unit), the Quoted-Price-difference, [[Price Flag]] direction, [[Justification]], and the Paper-Quote flag. Drafts stay out (they are private to their author, ADR-0011). Gated to **Analyst + Engagement Manager only** — never a [[Researcher]] (the Client Price is hidden from them, ADR-0003) and never an [[Admin]] (user-administration only) or [[Client User]]. Every successful run is **audited** (ADR-0018).
_Avoid_: Full export, admin export, staff dump

**Audit Event**:
One append-only record that a significant state transition happened: **who** (actor), **when** (timestamp), **what** ([[Audit Action]]), against **which subject** (entity type + id). The audit log is the single chronological stream of these events — internal-only, never on any client-facing path. The record can carry a **before/after** value pair for transitions that change a tracked monetary value; in v1 two actions write it: [[Client Price]] change ([[Benchmark Item]]) and a **manual rate override** on a [[Market Quote]] (`before` null, `after` the newly-pinned **document-total** [[Converted USD Price / USD Price per Unit|Converted USD Price]], summed across the document's lines — the raw [[Exchange Rate]] the analyst typed is recorded on the Market Quote itself, not in this generic monetary pair, whose precision is sized to a price). The pair stays null for every other action. Events are *written, never updated or deleted* — correcting a mistake is a new event, not an edit. Distinct from the row-level provenance some entities already self-record (a [[Quote Line]]'s latest `reviewedById`, a [[Country Release]]'s release stamps): those hold only the **latest** actor, whereas the Audit Event stream is the **full history**. Distinct too from the standalone [[Export Audit]] (ADR-0018), which records data *access*, not entity *change*.
_Avoid_: log line, history row, changelog, event (unqualified)

**Audit Action**:
The closed set of transitions the audit log records, each naming one move in the domain: **submit** (a [[Market Quote]]'s bulk submit — one event per document, subject [[Market Quote]]), **approve / reject** (an [[Analyst]]'s per-line verdict — subject [[Quote Line]]), **release / reopen** (a [[Country Release]] move), **import** (a bulk upsert of [[Benchmark Item]]s), **client-price-change** (an analyst sets or revises a [[Client Price]]), **manual-rate-override** (an [[Analyst]] hand-sets a [[Market Quote]]'s [[Exchange Rate]] for a currency the provider doesn't cover — subject [[Market Quote]], carries the document-total before/after pair), and **assign** (a [[Country Assignment]]). The subject types are MarketQuote, QuoteLine, BenchmarkItem, CountryRelease, CountryAssignment. An action not in this set is not audited in v1. Each action fixes the subject type it applies to and whether it carries a before/after value.
_Avoid_: event type, transition kind

**Notification**:
A single push signal telling one recipient that one significant thing happened — on the **only two v1 events that push** rather than wait to be pulled: a [[Quote Line]] **rejected** tells its **author** (the [[Researcher]] who created the parent document — *not* necessarily the item's [[Primary Researcher]]), and a [[Country]] **released** tells that tenant's [[Client User]]s. Every other domain change stays pull-only (no notification). A Notification reaches its recipient over **two channels at once** — email and an in-app inbox — and carries **never any [[Client Price]]** (ADR-0003). A **rejection** identifies the work in context — its [[Pricing Study]] name, [[Country]], [[Market Quote Number]] and [[Quote Line Number]] — plus the rejection reason, and links the author straight to the rejected line to revise it; a **release** names the study + country. Only **active** users are notified; a deactivated recipient is skipped on both channels. A release notifies **once per (study, country)** — the first time that pair is ever [[Released]]; a reopen and any subsequent re-release do *not* re-notify (a reopen is a pure visibility lever, nothing new to announce). An in-app **rejection** notification is **transient**: it is shown only while its [[Quote Line]] is still [[Rejected]] and is the line's latest rejection, so it disappears the moment the author **revises** the line (the issue resolved) and a later re-rejection shows only its own fresh notification. Email is fire-once and never retracted.
_Avoid_: alert, message, push (unqualified), in-app toast
